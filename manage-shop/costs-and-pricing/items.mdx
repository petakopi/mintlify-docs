---
title: "Items"
description: "Raw materials with pack price and yield — the building blocks of every recipe"
---

Items are the raw materials your recipes are built from: beans, milk, cups, lids, syrups, sugar. Each item carries a pack price and a yield %, and the app computes a per-unit cost from those two numbers.

## What you fill in per item

Pack size, pack price, and yield. That's it.

### Example: items for an Iced Coffee Latte

Throughout the docs we use **Iced Coffee Latte** as the running example. It's built from four items:

| Item | Pack size | Pack price | Yield | Computed unit cost |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Whole milk | 1,000 ml | RM 8.00 | 100% | RM 0.008 / ml |
| Coffee beans | 1,000 g | RM 80.00 | 90% | RM 0.0889 / g |
| PET cup | 1 cup | RM 0.30 | 100% | RM 0.30 / cup |
| Straw | 1 straw | RM 0.05 | 100% | RM 0.05 / straw |

The 90% yield on beans inflates the per-gram cost from RM 0.08 to RM 0.0889 — the app does this automatically so every recipe using those beans pays the real cost, not the on-pack cost.

<Info>**Yield matters when waste is real.** Coffee beans usually waste ~10% to grind and spillage. Set yield to 90% and the app applies the waste to your COGS automatically — every recipe that uses those beans pays the inflated unit cost, so a "cheap" bean with 70% yield can quietly cost more than a premium bean at 95%.</Info>

## Where items show up

- In every [Recipe](/manage-shop/costs-and-pricing/recipes) you build — each recipe line is `{Item, quantity}`, and the app sums the line costs to give you per-cup COGS.
- In the [Calculator](/manage-shop/costs-and-pricing/calculator)'s recipe-derived target line, indirectly: average COGS across recipes is what pushes the suggested price up or down.

If you change an item's price (supplier raised the bean price, packaging got cheaper), every recipe that uses that item recalculates automatically — there's no per-recipe re-save to chase.
